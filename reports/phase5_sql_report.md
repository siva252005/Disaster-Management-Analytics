# Overall Business Insights

* Floods are the most common disasters globally.
* Storms produce the highest economic losses.
* Earthquakes are responsible for the highest mortality.
* Asia remains the most disaster-prone continent.
* China and the United States experience the largest number of disasters.
* Bangladesh has recorded the highest cumulative deaths.
* The United States suffered the highest economic losses.
* Disaster occurrences increased sharply after the 1990s.
* Most disasters are classified as low severity, while a small number of severe events account for major human and economic losses.

---

# Conclusion

Analysis of historical disaster data from 1970 to 2021 reveals increasing disaster occurrences and substantial geographical variations. Floods and storms dominate global disaster frequency, while earthquakes account for the highest mortality. The results emphasize the importance of disaster preparedness, risk mitigation, and efficient resource allocation to reduce future losses and improve resilience.
